# About SkyMapper

The SkyMapper project was initiated in 2024 at KIST (Korea Institute of Science and Technology, a premier national research institute in South Korea). The primary goal of the project was to empower non-experts to easily and affordably build fully autonomous flying drones using only off-the-shelf commercial products and simple 3D-printed parts. 

While standard consumer drones are cheap, lightweight, and fun to fly, adapting them for industrial applications—such as targeted remote monitoring and 3D spatial scanning—typically requires deep technical expertise. Furthermore, achieving autonomous flight and high-precision mapping usually involves mounting heavy, expensive dedicated sensors like LiDAR, which drastically increases the overall cost and complexity. 

SkyMapper was born to overcome these exact limitations. By cleverly integrating the advanced Vision-Inertial Odometry (VIO) and processing power of everyday smartphones with commercial UAVs, we created a closed-loop control system that achieves professional-grade 3D reconstruction without the hefty price tag. We are dedicated to making high-end autonomous robotic capabilities accessible to everyone.
